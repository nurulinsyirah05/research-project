Modeling Lactic Acid Accumulation in Tempoyak Fermentation

Tempoyak is a traditional Malaysian fermented condiment crafted from durian pulp. Lactic Acid Bacteria (LAB), primarily Lactobacillus plantarum, ferment endogenous glucose and sucrose into lactic acid. This process drops the pH below 4.0, preserving the food against pathogens and imparting its characteristic sour aroma.

In industrial **Agro Product Technology (SFP)**, managing this biological system requires knowing not just the *amount* of acid at any time, but the **speed of acidification** (the rate of change). Calculating the mathematical derivative \(f'(x)\) allows food scientists to determine the exact moment of peak fermentation, schedule quality-assurance testing, and evaluate optimal batch harvest times.

🔬 Mathematical Kinetics

We model the cumulative organic acid accumulation using a Logistic Growth Function \(f(x)\). Its instantaneous derivative, \(f'(x)\), maps out the raw velocity of biological conversion (g/L/hour) across a 72-hour cycle.

Target Species: L. plantarum (LAB)
Substrate Matrix: D24 / Musang King Pulp
Product Output: Lactic Acid (g/L)

f(x) Chemical Calculus Formulas

Cumulative Lactic Acid Concentration Curve:
\[f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}\]
Instantaneous Fermentation Speed (Derivative):
\[f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)})^2}\]
\(L\) Max Acid Yield (g/L)
\(k\) Rate Constant (1/h)
\(x_0\) Mid-point (Hours)
